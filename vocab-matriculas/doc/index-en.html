Abstract

The purpose of this vocabulary is to describe in a semantic manner information related to university enrollments, including details regarding the enrollments for the following types of studies: degree, master and doctorate.

Introduction back to ToC

This vocabulary represents information related to university enrollments. The data used in it comes from a set of public spanish universities, more specifically, they belong to: the Automonous University of Madrid (UAM), the Carlos III University of Madrid (UC3M), the Complutense University of Madrid (UCM), the King Juan Carlos University (URJC), the University of Valladolid (UVa) and the University of Huelva (UHU). In this way, this vocabulary describes information about students which enroll in universities, enrolling in a given academic year. Furthermore, the university has a center and a set of degrees.

Moreover, it uses the geo and geosparql vocabularies to represent the geographical location of the center and the university associated to each enrollment, according to different coordinate reference systems commonly used for this type of data. Additionally, it reuses terms from the schema.org vocabulary for the representation of concepts and relations that are used to describe people. Finally, it also reuses the organizations vocabulary to represent the center and the university entities, which gathers all the enrollments that exist in it.

Vocabulary for the representation of university enrollments: Description back to ToC

Figure 1 shows the classes and properties of the vocabulary that represents the data of university enrollments:

As mentioned before, the scope of this vocabulary is the representation of data from university enrollments. To accomplish this purpose, as it can be seen in the figure, several classes and properties have been defined in order to describe these data, such as the Estudiante class, which collects information about the number of credits that the student possesses, his date of birth, his final average score (in case he has one), if the student graduates this year and if he gets any honorable mention. Furthermore, the Estudiante class has several SKOS concept schemes to represent his Final Degree Project score, his dedication, his gender and his master's degree type in case he possesses one. Besides, the following student information is registered for each enrollment: his nationality, residence and university provenance country (which is also represented by the vocabulary geosparql), continent and aggregation of countries, and his residence and course municipality (which is also represented by the vocabulary geosparql), province and autonomous community. Additionally, it has been reused the vocabulary schema:Person to describe the student. Finally, it has been also reused the vocabulary of org:Organization to show how the class Universidad has a suborganization, which is the Centro class. In this way, this class has been reused to explain that both mentioned classes (Universidad and Centro) have a geographical location, represented by the vocabulary geosparql.
