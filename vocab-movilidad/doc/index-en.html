Abstract

The purpose of this vocabulary is to describe in a semantic manner information related to university mobilities, including ingoing and outgoing mobilities. Specifically, this vocabulary collects information about students from other universities which enroll part of their studies in the university of reference, as well as information about students from the university of reference which enroll in other universities.

Introduction back to ToC

This vocabulary represents information related to university mobilities. The data used in it comes from a set of public spanish universities, more specifically, they belong to: the Automonous University of Madrid (UAM), the Carlos III University of Madrid (UC3M), the Complutense University of Madrid (UCM), the King Juan Carlos University (URJC), the University of Valladolid (UVa) and the University of Huelva (UHU). In this way, this vocabulary describes information about ingoing and outgoing mobilities. In it, a student possesses a provenance university as well as a destination university. Furthermore, the university has a set of degrees and a mobility programme.

Moreover, it uses the geo and geosparql vocabularies to represent the geographical location of the university where the student is studying the degree, which could be the provenance or destination university, depending on the mobility programme chosen, according to different coordinate reference systems commonly used for this type of data. Additionally, it reuses terms from the schema.org vocabulary for the representation of concepts and relations that are used to describe people. Finally, it also reuses the organizations vocabulary to represent the university entity, which gathers all the mobility programmes that exist in it.

Vocabulary for the representation of university mobilities: Description back to ToC

Figure 1 shows the classes and properties of the vocabulary that represents the data of university mobilities:

As mentioned before, the scope of this vocabulary is the representation of data from university mobilities. To accomplish this purpose, as it can be seen in the figure, several classes and properties have been defined in order to describe these data, such as the Estudiante class, which collects information about his date of birth, the starting month of the mobility programme and the ending month of the mobility programme. Furthermore, the Estudiante class has several SKOS concept schemes to represent his gender and his level of education. Besides, it is described for each student his nationality country (which is also represented by the vocabulary geosparql), continent and aggregation of countries. Regarding the other classes, the student studies in a university, which can be the provenance or destination university, depending on the mobility programme, provided by the university. Moreover, the provenance and destination universities possess a provenance and destination country (which is also represented by the vocabulary geosparql), continent and aggregation of countries respectively. However, the destination university also has a destination municipality (which is also represented by the vocabulary geosparql), province and autonomous community. Additionally, it has been reused the vocabulary schema:Person to describe the student. Finally, it has been also reused the vocabulary of org:Organization to show how the class Universidad has a geographical location, represented by the vocabulary geosparql.
